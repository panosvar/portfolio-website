Shared Sacred Space - Prophet Elijah's cave in Rizoupoli



Status:     Architectural Diploma in NTUA Masters degree

Program:     Community and Multi-faith Center

Year:     2019

Location:     N. Ionia, Athens, Greece

Team:     Panagiotis Varoutsos



Today, more than ever, the mass displacement of populations brings to the foreground the issue of dealing with multiculturalism. This is expressed through the day-to-day issues of both social management and religious identity. The integration of these two into a real spatial scenario is addressed by the present thesis through three sections of archiving, research-analysis and architectural design. The first section reveals an invisible network of informal places of worship outside the realm of the city's public life as well as the lack of a religious center of social reference. The cave of prophet Elijah in Rizoupoli is proposed as a place that responds to the common religious ground of these groups and provides a "safe place‚Äù in the urban environment. It offers an experience that strips each dogma of its socio-political implications and therefore any religious belief appears as an ideological behavior. The third section consists of the proposed architectural design that emerges through the sanctity of the cave at the level of public life as a social act. The sacred face of the cave in the city appears as a social concern through various amenities (KEP, library, catering) but also as a public relief through the square that functions as a neighborhood center. The building pervades the landscape of the city and is topologically structured as in a Moebius strip where the exterior and interior are two seemingly unrelated worlds: the physical -the city- and the sacred -the cave.
