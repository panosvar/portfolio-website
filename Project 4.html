A New Museum for the city of Athens



Status:     Academic-Architectural Design 7-8

Program:     Historic Museum at the city center

Year:     2017

Location:     Konstantinou Paparigopoulou 9, Klafthmonos Square, Greece

Team:     Panagiotis Varoutsos, Michalos Polyzos, Tina Papanikolaou



Situated on the historic centre of the city of Athens stands the museum of the city. The buildings that the museum is housed were the original palace of the first Greek monarch, King Othon Aâ€™ ,and were in use during 1836-1843. The present project acts as an expansion of the current complex in order to allow the museum experience and the more efficient artefact and exhibit exposition. The new building is operating as the link between the existing museum, the extension that is part of the proposal and the urban fabric. Simultaneously, its geometry and program of uses present the possibility of visual relationship between the city and the exhibition, rendering the city itself as part of the exhibition experienced during the visit to the museum. The building being as complex and vast as a museum is supposed to be was studied even further so as to solve a range of static and building material problems that have risen during the process.
