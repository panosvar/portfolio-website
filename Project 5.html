LowLine: Gateways to Different Worlds



Status:     RIBA Architectural Competition

Program:     Placeshaping for diversity along London's viaduct

Year:     2019

Location:     Lowline, London, England

Team:     Panagiotis Varoutsos, Anastasia Gkoliomyti



The proposal faces the challenge of residual spaces across the Victorian viaduct by assembling them into thematic clusters. Their character is drawn upon the existing traits of the neighborhood's uses and stakeholders, thus catering to the diverse communities and services available, while also introducing a variety of public and green spaces putting emphasis to pedestrian movements across the viaduct. The reinterpretation of the Old viaductâ€™s arches produces a symbolic architectural vocabulary of arch-derived geometries that serve as symbols of the historical importance of the railway infrastructure. These new arches, when intertwined with the old, produce an infrastructural space. For place-shaping to occur, the interior of the vaults is extended to the sidewalk, and vise versa. This architectural gesture of the arches extrusion to the public sphere creates a threshold between public and private spaces.
