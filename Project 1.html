The Anti-Factory: Towards a future of Sustainable Fashion



Status:     Academic-MSc1-2 Complex Projects

Program:     Reconstruction of Galleries Modernes as a fashion Hub

Year:     2022

Location:     Hoogstraat 179A, Rotterdam, Netherlands

Team:     Panagiotis Varoutsos, David Marcinek



Located in the centre of Rotterdam, the ‘Galeries Modernes’ was designed in the 1950’s as a staple of the new fordist economy. This post-war era consisted of a large economic boom with many new mass produced goods becoming available due to revolutions in mass production practises.Galeries Modernes is re-used and re-de-signed to become The “Anti-Factory”. “Anti” because it seeks to counteract the negative aspects of consumerism, but still “Factory” as it cannot function seperately from mass produced goods. On the contrary, it feeds of the excess of mass produced goods, transforming them into specialised goods. It undermines and opposes the factory also in the sense that the “design to break” philoshophy is actively fought against: by repairing, recycling and mending the act of buying completely new, mass produced clothing is what the building’s entire conception is trying to prevent.
